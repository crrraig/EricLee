Eric Lee is an experienced architectural designer with broad knowledge in exterior envelope, interior design and architectural details. He has accumulated extensive professional work experience in internationally recognized architectural design firms, mainly in New York City, in addition to experience in London, UK and Rotterdam, Netherlands. He has experience in all phases of architectural design from feasibility to construction administration work. His primary interest and experience is based and expanded from well thoughtout architectural design, ranging from conceptual design to detailed design. He is a graduate of University of Waterloo in Canada with an honor's degree in bachelor of architectural studies. He graduated with distinction and dean's honor list in 2013 with over two years of professional work experience from prestigious architectural firms in NYC, USA, Rotterdam, Netherlands and London, UK. Since 2013, he has been practicing architecture in commercial architecture firms in New York City. He holds professional credentials in LEED AP BD+C from USGBC and WELL AP from IWBI. Most recently, He received a specialized certification in Construction Management from Columbia University with graduate courses in construction project management and a certification in Healthier Building Materials and Sustainable Buildings from Parsons School of Design. He also holds certifications in Project Management from University of California and Renewable Energy and Green Building Entreneurship from Duke University. In addition to his dedicated interests and experience in feasibility, sustainable and detailed design work, he also holds interest and has recent experience in managing construction administration work. He is OSHA-10 certified. His past professional work experience includes Perkins+Will, FXCollaborative, RAMSA, Beyer Blinder Belle Architects & Planners, SOM, Pei Cobb Freed & Partners, OMA and KPF.
