Biography:

Eric Lee is an American-Canadian architectural design professional with over a decade of experience at internationally recognized design firms, primarily based in New York City, with additional professional experience in London, UK, Rotterdam, Netherlands and Portland, ME. His portfolio encompasses a wide range of project typologies—including high-end retail, mixed-use, commercial, residential, cultural, and educational work—across both new ground-up and adaptive reuse developments. He has worked on both interior and exterior architecture, contributing to numerous sensitive landmark restorations throughout New York City. His professional experience spans all phases of design and construction, from concept through construction administration, with a strong focus on thoughtful detailing, sustainability, and collaborative project delivery. Eric’s career includes full-time roles at prominent firms such as Robert A.M. Stern Architects, Pei Cobb Freed & Partners, SOM, KPF, OMA, FXCollaborative, and Perkins+Will. Most recently, he worked as a senior project designer at AYON Studio Architecture & Preservation, based in New York City, in addition to his previous role as senior architectural designer at SMRT Inc. in Portland, ME. He has experience leading both new ground-up and renovation projects of varying scales across the Greater New York and Tri-State area. His recent work has centered on adaptive reuse and urban renovation projects, including both interior and exterior adaptive-reuse projects. He also maintains a strong interest in real estate development and environmentally responsive design strategies that emphasize long-term value and investment for the future. Eric holds an Honours Bachelor of Architectural Studies from the University of Waterloo in Canada, where he graduated with distinction and Dean’s List recognition. Through the university’s co-operative education program, he gained over two years of full-time professional experience via international placements during his studies. Between 2021 and 2023, he furthered his education by completing several post-graduate certifications in Construction Management (Columbia University), Project Management (University of California), International Business (University of Colorado), and Sustainable Design and Affordable Housing (Parsons School of Design). He consistently maintains his professional credentials, including LEED AP BD+C (2014-current), WELL AP (2019-current), and Fitwel Ambassador (2024-current), along with holding an OSHA-10 construction safety training card (2020-current).He is a member of Associate AIA, New York Chapter.
